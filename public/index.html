TRANSFORMATIONS

Transformational geometry involves altering geometric shapes and figures to create new ones. The pre image, or the figure that will be transformed geometrically, is the input of a geometric transformation. The figure that results from the subsequent geometric transformation is then the image. Translation, rotation, scaling, and mirror reflection of a pre-image are examples of geometric transformations. Geometric transformations are essential, as they can be significantly applied in real life. This includes the simple utilization of rotating, translating, or dilating an object.
